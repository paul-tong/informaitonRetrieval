diogo co portuguese pronunciationdiou kw anglicised as diogo cam and also known as diego cam was a portuguese explorer and one of the most notable navigators of the age of discovery he made two voyages sailing along the west coast of africa in the 1480s exploring the congo river and the coasts of the present-day angola and namibia early life and family co was born in vila real some say in vora in the middle of the 15th century the illegitimate son of lvaro fernandes or gonalves co fidalgo of the royal household himself the illegitimate son of gonalo co he married and had four children pedro co manuel co andr afonso co and isabel co explorer he was the first european known to sight and enter the congo river and to explore the west african coast between cape st catherine in gabon and cape cross almost from the equator to walvis bay in namibia first voyage when king john ii of portugal restarted the work of henry the navigator he sent out co probably around midsummer 1482 to explore the african coast south of the equator he discovered the mouth and estuary of the congo probably in august 1482 and marked it with a padro or stone pillar erected on shark point attesting to the sovereignty of portugal this padro still stands to this day albeit in ruins he also ascended the great river for a short distance and commenced modest commerce with the natives of the bakongo kingdom co then coasted down along the present angola portuguese west africa and erected a second padro probably marking the termination of this voyage at cape saint mary the monte negro of these first visitors he returned to lisbon by 8 april 1484 where john ii ennobled him promoting him from esquire to a knight of his household and granted him an annuity and a coat of arms on his return he discovered the island of annobn second voyage that co on his second voyage of 14841486 was accompanied by martin behaim as alleged on the latters nuremberg globe of 1492 is very doubtful but it is known that the explorer revisited the congo and erected two more padro on land beyond his previous voyage the first was at cabo negro angola the second at cape cross the cape cross pillar probably marked the end of his progress southward some 1400 kilometers co ascended the kongo river which he thought led towards the realm of prester john up to the neighborhood of the site of matadi there in
